1.1 Reading the Data

mydata<-read.csv("College.csv", header=T, stringsAsFactors=TRUE)

The dataset(college.csv) contains 775 observations with 17 variables.Variable names are:

  1. Private A factor with levels Yes and No indicating private or public university.
  2. Apps Number of applications received.
  3. Accept Number of applications accepted.
  4. Enroll Number of new students enrolled.
  5. F.UG Number of full time undergraduates.
  6. P.UG Number of part time undergraduates.
  7. Outstate Out-of-state tuition (dollars).
  8. Room.Board Room and board costs (dollars).
  9. Books Estimated book costs (dollars).
  10. Personal Estimated personal spending (dollars).
  11. PhD Percent of faculty with Ph.D.students.
  12. Terminal Percent of faculty with terminal degree.
  13. S.F.Ratio Student/faculty ratio.
  14. alumni.deno Percent alumni who donate.
  15. Expend Instructional expenditure per student (dollars).
  16. Elite A factor with levels Yes and No indicating Elite university or not.
  17. Grad.Rate Graduation rate in percentage.

3.Improvement to the Linear Regression Model

  • Our analysis thus far has showed that:

    • Our residuals are not fully normally distributed
    • There exists high influential points which we might need to investigate further.
    • The Adjusted R-Squared of the best model with 5 predictors is low which indicates that a substantial portion of the variance still remains unexplained.

  • To improve the model, we will consider:

    • If there exists outliers in our model. We will use a box plot for this.
    • Transforming some of our predictors by taking log and square root transformations to address the non-normal distributions.
    • Explore interaction terms between independent variables. This might improve the adjusted R-Squared.
    • We will not consider a polynomial regression as our analysis has proved that our model is a linear fit.
x=boxplot(mynewdata$Apps, main = 'Apps')

y=boxplot(mynewdata$P.Undergrad, main = 'P.Undergrad')
